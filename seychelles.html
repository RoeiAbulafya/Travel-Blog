My Travels

Seychelles:

I discharged from the army during COVID-19, man that sucked! But then a glimpse of hope, a ray of sunshine came along. I saw a commercial that there are some flights to an exotic new destination: The Seychelles. I didn't know anything about that place but it sounded like the perfect oppurtunity to escape from the COVID madness. I texted my good friend from the army, Omer, and I offered him this place. He was sold and the jig was on! We met and started planning the trip, booked AirBnBs, we learned about the history of the place and were so excited. Finally, we were there and it was love from first sight! The beaches, the islands, the warm people, the hospitality, everything was perfect. We spent 2 wonderful weeks in The Seychelles and I just yearn to return. We came back to our country, to the harsh reality of the second quarantine but we will always remember this trip.

Picturesque Moments

My Seychelles Itinerary

  • Day 1: Arrival & Unpacking

    Arrived at Seychelles International Airport (SEZ) on Mahe Island. We rented a car at the airport and exchanged our money to SCR. We had to adjust to drive on the left side. Eventually, we got to Erica's apartment, a bit south from Victoria. Great apartment, Erica is the best. In the evening, we bought some groceriesfrom a nearby supermarket and went to the beach for a nightly swim.

  • Day 2: Victoria and good restaurants

    We travelled around Victoria, the capital. We ate some good food, went to the market to buy groceries, went to the botanical garden and almost got lost. In the evening, We sat at the best indian restaurant Iv'e ever dined in. This country is so cheap!

  • Day 3: Praslin & Vallee de Mai

    Took a ferry to Praslin Island. Visited the UNESCO World Heritage Site, Vallee de Mai, home to the unique Coco de Mer palm. Afternoon spent at Anse Lazio, one of the world's most beautiful beaches.

  • Day 4: La Digue & Iconic Beaches

    Day trip to La Digue by ferry. Rented bicycles to explore the island, visiting the famous Anse Source d'Argent with its granite boulders, and Grande Anse. Enjoyed fresh fruit juices by the beach. We met a cute dog and had a great milkshake in Bikini-Bottom!

  • Day 5: Curieuse Island & Giant Tortoises

    Boat excursion to Curieuse Island, a national park known for its giant Aldabra tortoises. Enjoyed a BBQ lunch on the beach and swam in the pristine bays. Returned to Praslin in the evening.

  • Day 6: Marine Park Snorkeling & Departure

    Morning spent snorkeling in the Sainte Anne Marine National Park, exploring vibrant coral reefs and marine life. Afternoon return to Mahe for departure from SEZ.

Travel Tips for Seychelles

  • Island Hopping: Don't stick to one island! Ferries and domestic flights make it easy to explore Mahe, Praslin, and La Digue.
  • Sun Protection: The equatorial sun is strong. Use high SPF sunscreen, wear hats, and seek shade during peak hours.
  • Local Currency: While Euros are widely accepted, having some Seychellois Rupees (SCR) is useful for smaller purchases and local markets.
  • Respect Nature: The Seychelles prides itself on conservation. Avoid touching coral, don't litter, and respect wildlife.
  • Water Shoes: Some beaches have coral fragments, so water shoes can be helpful for comfortable swimming and walking.
  • Creole Cuisine: Indulge in local dishes like fish curry, grilled fish, and fresh tropical fruits.
